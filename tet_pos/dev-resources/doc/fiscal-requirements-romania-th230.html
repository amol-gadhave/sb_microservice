This document is intended for UnifiedPOS based applications, in particular JavaOS, OPOS and POS for .NET applications. It is not intended for applications whihc are operating directly on fiscal printer device firmware API (for that see [rbc-cd]).

This document is derived from the counseling document initially provided by RBC [rbc-cd] and used by them during fiscal workshops. All fiscal constraints have to be checked with RBC in case of questions. This document provides information on how those constraints may be applied using the UnifiedPOS based FiscalPrinter implementations for JavaPOS, OPOS and POS for .NET provided through the Diebold Nixdorf Probase POS middle ware product.

Introduction

Romania is a fiscal country.

To cash-up money it is mandatory to use a solution homologated by the Romanian fiscal authorities. The homologation / fiscal certification will occur on the following elements:

  • The Fiscal Printer including Fiscal Controller, Electronic Journal ("EJ") and Data Communication Module ("CoMo");

  • The Printer Customer Display ("CD");

  • The Fiscal POS application

During the certification process it will be certified only the fiscal POS application together with the fiscal printer. The Back Store and Back Office software will not be part of this process. Sometimes the certification committee will check some values in back store/back office to the sure that the data was correctly synchronized and processed.

If the Customer Display, Printer, Electronic Journal, Transmission Module or the Fiscal controllers is out of service, the POS solution has to stop sales. It’s therefore forbidden to cash up.

The aim of the homologation / fiscal certification is to enable the Romanian Fiscal Authority (ANAF) to know the exact turnover performed by the retailers and therefore to know the corresponding VAT values (per VAT rates) that has to be collected. For these reasons in the certification procedure, will be tested the fiscal receipts, fiscal reports (including reports from Electronic Journal) and also will be tested Data Transmission to ANAF server.

Important

  • The dead-line enforced by the Romanian Fiscal Authorities are very tight and this leads to very short time for every step of this project:

    • Development

    • Testing

    • Fiscal certification

    • Pilot

    • Rollout of the new fiscal solution

  • In order to speed up the rollout to change the old fiscal solution with the new EJ fiscal solution, it is strongly recommended to continue to support the old fiscal printers in the new version of the fiscal POS application.

  • Supporting the old printers in new software version, will allow to do the rollout and deployment in time of working hours of the open store.

Currency

The currency in Romania is the LEU (used in retail is "Lei" as plural). It’s a currency with 2 decimals. The equivalent for "Cent" is called "BAN" The symbol used for this currency is "RON" or "LEI" but usually is used "Lei". The smallest bank note is 1 Lei (value = 0,22 EUR approximatively) Some western countries do not work with 1 cent (coin), and the lowest denomination is 5 cents (coin). From this reason, if the total of the receipt is 17,43 Euro, the Cash Register work in two ways:

Rounding the total to 17,45

Or

Rounding the change to the value divided by 5 cents.

In Romania it is not allowed to round any value (total or change), even if the cashier do not return the correct change to client.

Main Components of the Fiscal Printer HW Device

  • Receipt Printer

  • Fiscal Controller

  • Fiscal Memory

  • Electronic Journal (EJ)

  • Data Communication Module – Data Transmission Terminal to ANAF (CoMo)

The last two components (EJ and CoMo) are introduced in the new fiscal law, and will be subject to a specific and complex set of tests.

Note
 Compared with the old fiscal printer with two paper roles, the new one, MF-TH230+ RO has only one paper role for customer receipts. The journal paper role was replaced by Electronic Journal.
Tip
 Because a Journal paper doesn’t exists anymore, it is recommended for the fiscal POS application to keep the history of the sales and tickets done by the printer, in order to be used by the operator to search and find specific tickets. This journal should be available on the back store server.

Printer Sensors

It is recommended to register call back function for receiving StatusUpdateEvents and that way being asynchronously informed about the paper and cover status. See your according UnfiedPOS documentation (appendix A, B and C) how to register such call back functions.

The initial paper and cover status reflecting the HW sensor status are send just after device enabling.

Paper Near End

When paper becomes near end, the PAPER LED of the printer will turn yellow. It detects when the paper is almost ended. The receipt can be closed (as long as MISSING PAPER sensor is not ON). After current receipt is closed, another one can’t be open until new paper is put into the printer. When PAPER NEAR END is triggered during fiscal ticket, the fiscal POS application has two possibilities:

  • Cancel the ticket which is in progress using resetPrinter or clearError methods, show a message on operator display to change the paper role and after a new paper role is put into the printer, the ticket will be reprinted from the beginning (this is usually specific for hypermarkets environment where the ticket can be very long)

  • Or, the printing/sales can continue until it will be closed (if MISSING PAPER is not triggered before this). After the ticket is closed, the POS application will show a message on operator display.

The StatusUpdateEvent received for such situations is FPTR_SUE_REC_NEAREMPTY.

Missing Paper

If paper is missing, the PAPER LED turns yellow and ERROR LED on the printer will turn RED. The fiscal POS application must detect this status and must stop sales/ printing. A message must be displayed on cashier display. Paper needs to be put into the printer and after this the ticket must be canceled and reprinted from the beginning. The reason of this request is that it is not allowed by the fiscal law to have a fiscal ticket on two or more pieces of paper.

The StatusUpdateEvent received for such situations is FPTR_SUE_REC_EMPTY.

After paper change it is recommended to perform a resetPrinter to get fiscal status of the fiscal device reseted before proceeding sales.

Note
 The paper status can also be obtain via properties RecPaperEmpty and RecPaperNearEnd on by polling. However, this is not recommended. Use Status update events instead.

When Missing Paper sensor will be triggered, the current printed receipt goes internally in an interrupted status and becomes invalid. The only way out from this status is to call resetPrinter or clearError methods.

Cover Open

Detects if the printer cover is open. A opened cover will automatically interrupt the current running fiscal receipt if any is ongoing. Due to the printer HW technology a cover open status and the missing paper status will be entered the same time.

The StatusUpdateEvent received for such situations is FPTR_SUE_COVER_OPEN.

The fiscal POS application must detect this status by processing the according StatusUpdateEvent FPTR_SUE_COVER_OPEN and must stop sales/ printing. Printing will continue when the cover will be closed and the paper roll is inserted.

Power Interruption Check

The printer may be powered by the Beetle or by a separate external power supply.

  1. If the printer is powered from Beetle, only the recovery after power off will be tested (printer goes offline together with the Beetle)

  2. If the printer is powered on from the external power supply, that will require three set of tests for power off during each area of the ticket:

    1. Only for printer powered off. Fiscal POS application must stop the sales. It’s not allowed to continue sale without the printer online;

    2. Only Beetle will be powered off;

    3. Both printer and Beetle powered off.

Fiscal Controller

Fiscal Print Restrictions

  1. It is not possible to sell an article with price zero – it will return error.

  2. It is not possible to sell an article with an empty article name.

  3. It is not possible to sell an article with quantity zero

Important
 All those restrictions will be checked by the fiscal device and the print attempt will be rejected. However, the fiscal POS application must check before these restrictions and react with a warning on the operator display. It is required by the law to check before calling the appropriate printRecItem method and it is recommended to show an error message on the operator display.
Note
 Also for buffer printing this is required, because otherwise you will receive the error from the printer after scanning all the items – this will require rescanning all the items.

Printer can be used in "scan and print" or in "buffer print" mode.

On each first beginFiscalReceipt call when fiscal day is still closed (DayOpened = false) a new EJ file is opened. All the fiscal and non-fiscal tickets done during the fiscal day will be stored in an fiscal device internal EJ file (without graphical information). Please note that the reports printed on the paper with data from fiscal memory or EJ will not be stored in EJ file. A call `printZReport`will close the EJ file.

It is allowed to print fiscal sales tickets with total ticket amount of zero.

Fiscal Requirements

Internal calculations on printRecItem calls are done using 3 decimals, the values are rounded to two decimals, subsequently, when they are printed on the receipt. This has especially to be considered in case of measured or weight items. The fiscal POS application must use the same way of calculation in order to avoid differences on the total amount passed to printRecTotal or printRecSubtotal calls.

Note
 There will be two special tests (cross checking) to be performed – they can be found in chapter Special Procedures.

POS application will use a special procedure for validation of transaction in case the fiscal ticket was closed – it can be found in chapter Special Procedures.

Storing VAT Rates

In difference to the old fiscal printers, the new EJ based fiscal printer TH230 has 2 additional VAT rates (VAT G and VAT H). POS application must synchronize the VAT rates from back store server to POS application and also to fiscal printer using the methods setVatValue and setVatTable.

The new fiscal law and within this new fiscal printer allows to set up to 8 different VAT rates. VAT rates E and F (respectively index 4 and 5) are always zero; VAT rates G and H (respectively index 6 and 7) may be set to "unused" by setting them to 99,99%. Up to 30 VAT table changes (by applying setVatTable) are accepted by the fiscal printer. Two decimal values are considered implicit when passing a value to setVatValue.

Reprinting Tickets from Electronic Journal

POS application must have the capability to:

  • Search and reprint of tickets from Electronic Journal by Date Time and Z Report number (AKA Session). See directIO EJ_REPRINT* for details.

  • Request different EJ Reports from the fiscal printer and save them as virtual printout on PC/Beetle. See chapter "Soft Copy Report" in the Javadoc OVERVIEW document [jd-ov].

The POS application must have the option to set up the path where these files will be stored on PC/Beetle and server to be available for investigation on server display. See the appropriate directIO MAKE_SOFT_COPY in the Javadoc documentation [jd-ro].

In case the EJ reprint has to be stored on an external storage connected to CoMo, the string "_EXTERNAL_STORAGE_" must be passed as file to the directIO MAKE_SOFT_COPY.

All soft-copy files are stored as text files.

Managing the Data Communication Module to ANAF

The protocol of transfer data from fiscal printer to ANAF server will be via XML files.

The XML file will be created in Communication Module from the data received from fiscal controller. The data can be information related to the tickets, Z Reports, events, etc.

Data transmission to fiscal authority (ANAF) can work in online mode, or in offline mode. If the printer loses the connection to ANAF server, it will switch to offline mode. In offline mode, all the XML files generated by the fiscal printer (data communication module), must be downloaded with digital signature by the fiscal POS application in order to be send to ANAF using another way of communication.

It is the user responsibility to transmit offline data to ANAF within maximum number of days (parameter of communication profile). If in this period the offline data was transmitted to ANAF, a password will be received from fiscal authority in order to reset the counter of days. If data is not send within setting days, the printer will be blocked for sales.

Details will be provided by RBC.

POS application must manage:

  • offline connection to ANAF server – check the status of fiscal file transfer with fiscal printer and display a warring message upon cashier sign on Message example: "Offline for 37 hours!"

  • offline connection to ANAF server – check the status of fiscal file transfer with fiscal printer and display a warring message upon cashier sign on Message example: "Offline for 25 days! At 60 days POS will be blocked for sales" Messages must require Supervisor acknowledge!

More information will be available after the publishing of the data transmission rules and protocol. According directIO commands will be added

Fiscal Memory

The fiscal memory works only in adding mode. It’s not possible to decrease/subtract values from the fiscal memory. From this reason, it’s impossible to void a fiscal transaction if the fiscal receipt was printed. In some POS applications exists a function for CANCEL ticket after the ticket was closed. In Romanian fiscal environment, this function is FORBIDDEN and can’t be available on the cashier keyboard or touch screen.

It is also forbidden to void an article that was not previously sold on the fiscal ticket.

Also, it is forbidden to use the function return and refund article on the fiscal receipt. For this functions is a special procedure described below in the document.

The fiscal memory as well as the EJ storage are finite. Therefore, the application must be able to handle Fiscal Memory Near Full and Full and EJ Near Full and Full situations.

The MF memory has 16.384 blocks at the TH230 Romania. The fiscal memory is considered nearly full if less or equal 60 free blocks remains. The remaining fiscal memory may be read out from property RemainingFiscalMemory. It returns the number of remaining blocks!

Fiscal Memory is Full

When the fiscal memory becomes full the fiscal POS application must display a message on the operator display related to this situation. It is forbidden to sale if the fiscal memory is full. Message example: "Fiscal Memory is full. Call service support!"

In case of Fiscal Memory Full, the reporting from MF and EJ is mandatory to be available.

Tip
 At each fiscal day begin (first beginFiscalReceipt when fiscal day is closed), the POS application should check the memory status from property RemainingFiscalMemory.

Electronic Journal is Full

When the EJ becomes nearly full and full, the fiscal POS application must display a message on the operator display related to this situation.

The application may stay informed about the status of the EJ by 2 ways:

  1. Either, by register a callback for receiving DirectIOEvents.

  2. Or, by polling the EJ status calling the directIO DIRECTIO_EJ_GET_STATUS

The following DirectIOEvents may be received:

directIOEvent(1100/EJ_OK, data, obj)

The EJ has enough memory. Parameters "data" and "obj" are not used.

directIOEvent(1101/EJ_NEARLY_FULL, data, obj)

The remaining free space on the EJ medium is nearly full. The POS operations may be continued. Parameters "data" and "obj" are not used.

directIOEvent(1102/EJ_FULL, data, obj)

Almost no space left on the EJ medium. Only specific operations are allowed to complete running fiscal documents and to close the fiscal day. After having completed these operations an change of the EJ medium is absolutely needed. In case fiscal day is opened, no fiscal document opened: Only a call to printZReport is allowed. During a running fiscal receipts: The running fiscal receipt must cancelled, only calls to resetPrinter or clearError() are allowed. Parameters "data" and "obj" are not used.

An initial directIOEvetn about the EJ status is also issued during device enabling.

Tip
 At each fiscal day begin (first beginFiscalReceipt when fiscal day is closed), the POS application should check the EJ status calling directIO directIO DIRECTIO_EJ_GET_STATUS, see Javadoc document for details [jd-ro].

Customer display

Even if the fiscal law is changed, the old Wincor Nixdorf Customer Display 2x20 characters installed, can be used from hardware and software perspective. The printer customer display has to display 2 lines x 20 characters. The POS application must handle the display.

The POS application is responsible to display the following information:

  • When an item is scanned, sales information must be shown on the Customer Display.

  • Total line (balance due) must be printed on Customer Display.

  • Each Payment should be displayed on Customer Display and the Balance Due must be refreshed.

  • It is mandatory for the change value to be displayed on Customer Display until the printing of the ticket ends (close a cash drawer).

  • If using sales printing in buffering mode, the items will be displayed on Customer Display only when they are scanned. It’s not necessary to display them again when the ticket will be printed, otherwise it will slow down the printer.

Important
 It is FORBIDDEN to sell if the Customer Display is disconnected from the fiscal controller. The Customer Display must be connected to fiscal printer’s serial port. It is not allowed to use a customer display connected to Beetle. If the Customer Display is not connected to the Fiscal Printer and the application try to open a fiscal receipt, the printer reacts with an error/exception EFPTR_MISSING_DEVICES.

Fiscal Ticket - General Points

Ticket Header

Graphical Logo Header

  • Fiscal printer has the capability to store and print the graphical logo (e.g. company logo). See chapter "Graphic Printing" in the Javadoc document for details [jd-ov].

Fiscal Header

  • the fiscal header will be printed automatically by fiscal printer

  • it is mandatory for each receipt/ticket and reports

  • normally, iIt will be in charge of Authorized Service Company to setup the fiscal header using the service tool.

  • Fiscal Header has 3 to 5 lines (44 characters per line), see method `setHeaderLine´

  • Lines 1, 2 and 5 are mandatory. Lines 3 and 4 are optional – if they are blank, they will be nor printed.

    • Line 1 – Company Name

    • Line 2 – Address of the store

    • Line 3 and line 4 – extensions for address, if needed.

    • Line 5 – CIF of the company

      1. First 25 characters from Line 5 can be modified. Example: "Cod Fiscal C.I.F.:"

      2. The next 2 characters will be Fiscal Attribute: "RO" or " "

      3. The next 13 characters will be for Company CIF

      4. The fiscal attribute and CIF will be automatically extracted by fiscal printer from fiscal serial number set during fiscalization

  • The fiscal header can be changed 16 times. (in the old law, changes were not allowed); After this, Fiscal Memory and EJ SD Card must be replaced with new ones in order to allow other changes. If CIF of the company will be changed, Fiscal Memory and EJ SD Card must be replaced.

Additional header lines

  • Fiscal POS application may set the property AdditionalHeader for setting additional header lines. E.g. like "store working hours", hello messages, etc.

  • multiple lines must be separated by NL characters. It is forbidden to use digits at columns 33 to 44;

Sales Area

This is the area of the receipt where the sold items are printed.

It starts with printing of the currency (LEI) on the right side of the ticket, before first sales line. This is in charge of the fiscal printer to be printed. For each item sold, the POS application have to call the method printRecItem passing the article description, the unit price, the measurement unit, the quantity of the article, the VAT rate ID and group id the article belongs to. The article group (AKA article category) must be passed as postfix to the article description.

All printRec* method calls require a article category number to passed alongside the description. As the standard does not provide such an information to be passed as separate argument, the article category number must be append to the ordinary article description separated trough a colon sign ':'. The article category will not be printed but registered inside the fiscal printer to be sent to the authorities on request. The article category is between 1 and 7 digits long. If not provided the default article category number zero is assumed.

Note
 The articles categories must come from the cack office like a separate field in data base of articles. The retailer must take in consideration the modification of data base of articles according with the nomenclature of article categories. RBC can provide this nomenclature.

For each item sales lines may be accompanied by a post line (e.g. comments, article name in other language, article EAN digits code, etc.). Set the property PostLine accordingly before printRecItem is called.

The article price will be calculated by the fiscal controller from the given quantity and unit price with 3 decimals. It will rounded to 2 decimals before printed on the fiscal receipt. In order fit the total ticket amount accumulated by the fiscal printer device, the POS application has to calculate article price the same way and add it to the application internal ticket amount totalizer.

For weight items a quantity with 3 decimals accuracy (exception to this rule are the fuel items which must use an accuracy of 5 decimals) may be passed. The same calculation and rounding rules apply as above.

At the end of the ticket, the application will provided the application calculated tikezt total amount by passing it to the method printRecTotal. If this total amount does not match the total amount calculated internally by the fiscal printer device, it will cancel the receipt (it is mandatory that the POS reads the current receipt total from the fiscal controller, see getTotalizer method, before calling the printRecTotal method).

Important
 It is not allowed to do sales on group/department. Only sales per/as item are allowed!

Promotions are allowed by

  • Promo messages → printRecMessage

  • Promo on Item → printRecItemAdjustment

  • Promo on sub total → printRecSubtotalAdjustemen

  • Group Promotion / Mix and Match Promotions will be handled just as sub total promotions

  • Fiscal POS application must calculate and send to the fiscal printer the value of the discount (even for Percentage Discount)

Loyalty cards are allowed to be used in order to get promotions on item or on ticket total.

Surcharges are allowed (e.g.: additional cost for night sales, for cold drinks, etc.), the same way as discounts. See UnifiedPOS standard for details.

International rounding rules:

  • <0,5 → 0

  • ≥0,5 → 1

The new fiscal printer device allows selling fractions of a boxed article (e.g. pills).

Note
 Selling boxed articles is currently not implemented by Probase POS' FiscalPrinter.

Total Area

Total area contains:

  • TOTAL LINE printed in double high, double width

  • Detailed ticket VAT values per VAT rates (printed automatically by the fiscal printer device)

Total area information and values are printed by the fiscal printer device when receiving the first call to the method printRecTotal.

Important

Mandatory Cross Checking of Ticket Total:

  • In order to be sure that Ticket total amount is correct, the POS application must read it from the fiscal printer device calling getTotalizer with the appropriate arguments (see UnfiedPOS specification for details).

  • If the value read from the printer is different from the value calculated by the POS application, the ticket must be canceled.

  • An error message must be shown on operator display - message "Fiscal Total Amount ≠ Total POS Amount".

  • No reprint in this situation – a reprint in this case goes to loop!!!
Note
 After TOTAL line, the fiscal ticket enters the "Tender Area" (payment area) and cannot go back in the sales area if the customer wants to buy more articles or if the customer has no money to cover the total value if the ticket. From this reason, the management of payment until end of ticket must be in charge of POS application software and the calls to printRecTotal will be issued only when the total incoming money is greater than the total ticket value.

Tender Area / Media Payment Area

The fiscal law requires registering of all kinds of payment amounts applied per receipt, per day and per whole fiscal printer life time. It holds according totalizers accumulating these values. Therefore this fiscal printer has new and special constraints how printRecTotal may be applied for register those payments. For that a generic payment list is maintained by the fiscal printer which may be partially altered by the application to register own payment types which later on may be used at printRecTotal calls. For that several PAYMENT_LIST directIOs has been defined. Please refer to directIO description in the Javadoc document [jd-ro].

For registering the applied payment value to a specific payment type counter the first character of the payment description in printRecTotal calls must be an integer value from 0 to 19 followed by an colon ":"; the tail of the payment description string is ignored. The integer value corresponds to the entry index in the PAYMENT_LIST partially maintainable by the application and has the following meaning:

  • 0 - cash

  • 1 - card

  • 2 - food stamp

  • 3 - voucher

  • 4 – bon value

  • 5 - credit

  • 6 - cash back (cannot be applied at printRecTotal; see Proposal for a Cash Back Implementation for details)

  • 7 - modern payment method

  • 8 - other payment

  • 9..19 – application defined payment; maintained via PAYMENT_LIST directIOs (see directIO for details)

If no integer value is given or colon is omitted, cash payment is assumed.

After the change due has been printed, the ticket may contain additional messages. E.g. thanks’ messages; promotion and marketing messages; contact information, etc.

It is recommended to print en empty line after the change due line before the additional messages start (use printRecMessage for that).

  • POS number

  • Cashier ID or Name

  • Transaction ID from POS application – it’s recommended

  • Store number – it’s recommended

  • It is recommended to have one blank line before fiscal trailer, again.

Fical Ticket Footer

The fiscal ticket footer is in charge to be printed by the fiscal printer, after it receives a call to method endFiscalReceipt. It contains the following information:

  • EJTRZ – transaction ID per day from EJ journal (this number may be obtained by an appropriate call to getData, see Javadoc document [jd-ro] for details).

  • Unique ticket id code ID UNIC - fiscal serial number + ticket date and time + number of fiscal day + number of fiscal ticket

  • Number of fiscal ticket (from the printer side) – this number is reset to one after each Z report. This number might be different from the transaction ID from POS application

  • Data and Time

  • A line with logotype "RL" and fiscal serial number (SNO)

  • A line with text "B O N F I S C A L"

After the fiscal footer is printed, the printer will automatically cut the paper/ticket.

There is an option print additional information behind the fiscal footer (in particular behind the text "B O N F I S C A L"). For that the application has to set the property AdditionalTrailer accordingly as described in the UnifiedPOS standard.

Important

Validation of fiscal ticket in fiscal printer and POS application

  • At the end of the ticket, a cross checking is mandatory to be done by POS application.

  • For every fiscal ticket, the POS application must be sure if the fiscal ticket was registered in fiscal printer in order to validate the transaction in its database, otherwise it will lead to daily values differences between fiscal Z Report and POS application.

  • It is recommended to fetch the day totalizers and counters using appropriate calls to getTotalizer and getData respectively. If totalizers and counters had been increased accordingly, the fiscal ticket was successfully stored in fiscal printer’s memory and the transaction must be valid.

  • If totalizers and counters had not been increased accordingly, the fiscal ticket was not entered into fiscal printers fiscal memeory and POS application must check the fiscal printer’s status and void the ticket if needed. Please be aware that the fiscal tickets with zero value will not increment the day totalizer but the day receipt counter.

Media Payments

Fiscal Printer Wincor Nixdorf MF-TH230+ RO for Romania, works with following media payments:

  • Default: 9 media payments (can’t be modified or deleted)

    • Cash (ro: CASH) – ID 0

    • Credit / Debit / Food Stamps Card (ro: CARD) – ID 1

    • Food Stamps (ro: TICHETE) – ID 2

    • Voucher (ro: VOUCHER) – ID 3

    • Discount Stamps (ro: BON/TICHET VALORIC) – ID 4

    • Retail CREDIT (ro: CREDIT) - ID 5

    • Cash Back (ro: AVANS IN NUMERAR) - ID 6

    • Modern Payment Methods (ro: METODE MODERNE de PLATA) - ID 7

    • Other Modern Payment Methods (ro: ALTE METODE MODERNE de PLATA) - ID 8

  • Additional 11 Optional Media Payments: ID 9 to ID 19

  • Optional Media Payments are programmable using appropriate directIO below, see Javadoc document [jd-ro] for details.

    • Can be defined – directIO SET_PAYMENT_LIST

    • Can be updated – directIO SET_PAYMENT_LIST

    • Can be erased – directIO SET_PAYMENT_LIST_VOID

    • Changes to Optional Media Payment definitions are allowed only when the fiscal day is closed

For every definition and update of an Optional Media Payment, an Info Ticket is printed. For deletion of an optional Media Payment, NO info ticket will be printed.

During sales / fiscal tickets, it’s the cashier’s responsibility to register the media payments according to what was received from the customer.

Important
 Because each media payment has its own ID inside the fiscal printer and for the default media payments it can’t be changed and, on the other hand, inside the POS application other media payments IDs are used / required, it might be needed to implement a mapping table for media payments, in order to make the correct links between media payments from POS application to media payments from fiscal printer.

The fiscal printer and also the law do not allowed to give back food stamp or gift voucher to the customer as change due.

Food stamps (food vouchers) can be used by customer only to buy food products. In order to check the payment, it is recommended that POS application keep the count for each food item sold (if item data base contain one field for food or non-food articles) that can be paid with food stamp. The cashier can first scan all the food items and use sub total methods in order to have the total value of food articles. Payment in advance

  1. In Romania is required to make a fiscal ticket and invoice for payment in advance.

  2. It is recommended to create an article named "Avans" with variable price.

  3. Because it is not possible to return Advance Payment using the fiscal devices, a special procedure must be applied:

    • For the value which was paid, the cashier will give to customer a fiscal ticket with article "Avans" and an invoice.

    • When the customer will come to the store to pick up the products ordered, the store will proceed in one of the two possible ways:

      1. First possibility (recommended):

        1. Cancel the total value of the payment in advance. For this will be used a procedure for return article "Avans" and issue a negative invoice

        2. After the "Avans" was canceled, the customer and the cashier can scan the articles and pay like a normal sale fiscal receipt. At request of the customer an invoice can be done.

      2. Second possibility:

        1. First step is that the cashier to scan all the articles ordered in advance

        2. On the same fiscal ticket, the method printRecRefund may be used for the returning the item "Avans" (value will be printed on the ticket with minus sign). In this situation the customer will pay only the difference between the total value of articles and the payment in advance.

Note
 the POS application must check the total value of ticket to be positive (>=0). In case the total value goes to negative, it will be necessary to follow the procedure described as First Possibility in order to cancel the Advance Payment. At request of customer an invoice will be issued.
Note
 It is recommended to use the first procedure in order to obtain all the time a positive fiscal receipt. In both situations the POS application can have a media payment named "Avans Platit" (English: Advance Paid), which will be used instead of using cash.

Article Return

  • According to the Romanian Fiscal Law, fiscal printer cannot register negative amounts into fiscal memory. From this reason a special procedure will be used for Return of Articles.

  • The Romanian Fiscal Law explain on art. 37: In case of RETURN of goods, the user made a Paper File with the followings:

    1. Note of reception (cod 14-3-1A), on the base of fiscal receipt issued in the past for goods (when they were sold).

    2. Payment Order to central cashier (cod 14-4) used to prove that the customer received his money back.

    3. Mandatory: Invoice or Invoice in RED (Negative Value) in order for the retailer to collect the VAT for returned articles.

  • When an article is returned, a fiscal document has to be generated: a negative invoice (Invoice in Red).

  • It isn’t possible to print on the fiscal printer a fiscal receipt with a negative value ⇒ an invoice with negative quantities, value, total and VAT has to be generated, even if the client doesn’t want the invoice.

  • All these return invoices will be collected in the accounting department and are the base of returned VAT and 394 monthly tax declaration.

  • 2 documents (Payment Order and Note of Reception) are generated on the laser printer or fiscal printer as non-fiscal tickets. Error Handling

During the execution of fiscal methods printer errors may happen (paper jam, cut error, cover open, etc.). The POS application must recover after any error getting its internal status up to date regarding the fiscal status. A precondition for that is to take care of the fiscal ticket status (property PrinterStatus).

  • To do handle errors the application may

    • take care of exceptions/error codes returned on particular fiscal method calls (accompanied with calls to directIO GET_MF_ERROR if needed)

    • call clearError in case of logical fiscal printer errors. Such error are typically reported back through E_ILLEGAL errors/exceptions. In that case, usually, the ErrorCodeExtended property contains the fiscal printer device error which would also be returned by the directIO GET_MF_ERROR.

    • call resetPrinter to get out of error situations caused by receipt printer errors (e.g. paper errors, cover errors, cutter errors, mechanical errors). Such errors are typically reported back by general E_FAILURE errors/exceptions. In that case, usually, the ErrorCodeExtended property contains the fiscal printer device error which would also be returned by the directIO GET_MF_ERROR.

    • However, there may be FiscalPrinter category specific errors reported as E_EXTENDED errors. In that case the ErrorCodeExtended property has to be checked for the detailed error code (see UnifiedPOS standard, chapter "FiscalPrinter → ErrorEvent" for details).

    • It is recommended to log the error message provided through property ErrorMessage [JavaPOS, POS for .NET only; n/a for OPOS]. Especially during development it contains helpful hints about the error situation. During development it also may be quite helpful to enable the driver logging facilities (see "JavaPOS Error Logging" on how to configure and where to find), as the logging file may contain hints on how to come out of a particular error situation.

  • To prevent errors the application must

    • avoid negative sales. No return article receipt is allowed.

    • check the possible usage errors through the cashier and react with a message before calling the appropriate fiscal method. E.g., if one piece of a specific item was sold at the fiscal ticket, but after this, the cashier tries to void 3 pieces of the same item, the POS application must show an error message and do not call the appropriate voiding method.

    • check all examinable law constraints before calling appropriate fiscal methods. We recommend to show an appropriate error message on the operator display in such cases.

    • Also in case of buffer printing it is required to check the law constraints just after the articles has been scanned, because otherwise the application may receive an error post scanning during receipt printout which may required to rescan all items.

  • To prevent errors the application may

    • take care of StatusUpdateEvents.

    • use particular getTotalizer and getData method calls to take care of the counter and totalizer status of the printer.

    • call checkHealth on a regular manner (e.g. before each fiscal day begin) to check the health status of the fiscal device.

Training Mode

  • The new fiscal printer has supports a training mode

  • The training mode may be activated only after the fiscal printer has been fiscalized and before the first fiscal day has been opened.

  • In training mode all the tickets and Z Reports are not registered to fiscal memory or electronic journal.

The way of working is the following:

  1. After the fiscalization is completed the method beginTraining have to be called.

  2. While the fiscal printer is in training mode, the application may open and end a fiscal day and print receipts as usual for training purposes without limits.

  3. All the printed tickets and reports have the same layout as the production tickets. For this this reason it is highly recommended to print an appropriate message in front of each fiscal ticket which will shows that the fiscal printer is in training mode. E.g., "MOD SCOLARIZARE. BON DE SCOLARIZARE. BON NEFISCAL" (eng.: Training Mode. Training Ticket. Non-Fiscal Ticket)

  4. When the training session is completed, the fiscal printer must be switched to production mode by calling endTraining.

Note
 After the training has ended and the first fiscal day has been opened, the fiscal printer device will not allow another training session. Each call to beginTraining will be rejected with E_ILLEGAL.

Fiscal Ticket Consistency Check [MANDATORY]

A validation of of fiscal ticket status between fiscal printer, POS application and transaction back office store server is required.

  1. At the end of the ticket, a cross checking is mandatory to be done by POS application.

  2. For every fiscal ticket the POS application must be sure, whether the fiscal ticket was registered successfully at the fiscal printer device in order to validate the transaction in its database. Otherwise, it will lead to daily totalizer amount differences between fiscal Z Report and POS application database.

  3. This may be archived by fetching the day totalizer and counters using appropriate call to getTotalizer and getData methods (see Javadoc document [jd-ro] for details). If the day counter are incremented accordingly after ticket had been finished, the fiscal ticket was successfully registered in the fiscal printer device and the transaction is valid.

  4. If counters and totalizers has not been incremented accordingly, the fiscal ticket was not registers and the POS application must check the status of the fiscal printer device and mark its internal ticket status as invalid.

Note
 Please take into consideration a fiscal ticket may have a total amount of zero. In this case getTotalizer has to be fetched to check the constraint mentioned above.

24 hours Z Report [MANDATORY]

It’s not allowed to sale when 24 hours have passed since first fiscal ticket of the day.

The 24 hours period is calculated from the first fiscal ticket.

  • NOT from the first non-fiscal ticket issued for whatever reason (like cashier sign on, etc.)!

  • NOT from the point in time the fiscal day has been opened!

Usually, after 24 hours POS application must prompt on operator display a message regarding this situation "24 hours elapsed, Z Report is required!" A call to printZReport must be performed (usually in manually triggered) before sales can be continuted.

In order to calculate 24hours period the application may * remember the time stamp of first fiscal receipt, or * may use the directIO GET_DATE_FIRST_RECEIPT which returns the time stamp of first fiscal relevant receipt

Synchronization of Date and Time [MANDATORY]

Date and Time has to be synchronized between POS application and Fiscal Printer using the method setDate, see UnifiedPOS standard for details.

Date and Time must be synchronized for the entire chain in the following order: back office store server → fiscal POS application → fiscal printer.

Note
 Taking into consideration that fiscal data must be sent to Romanian Fiscal Authority server, in real time and the time stamp of the files is important, it is recommended to have the date and time of the back office store server synchronized with a public available time server.

Regarding setting the date and time in fiscal printer:

  • Each block of fiscal memory contains a date and a time-stamp.

  • Date and time of fiscal printer can’t be set to an earlier date and time than the last block stored at fiscal memory (usually, the last EOD Z report).

  • Date and time of fiscal printer can be set only for a point in the future. As a protection against accidentally set wrong date in future, the fiscal printer allows a new date and time with a maximum of two days in the future.

  • Date and time of fiscal printer must be set only if fiscal day is closed. That means the POS application must set the date and time before the first legally relevant receipt (fiscal sales receipt or generic fiscal receipt) is started.

HINT: To fulfill the above request, fiscal day should not be started right after Z Report. Fiscal day should be opened when the cashier logging in to the till. This allows POS application to have a time gap to synchronize the date and time. E.g., printZReport may have been called at 23:00, then the fiscal day should be started at 8:00 at the next morning.

When the fiscal POS application is going to start the fiscal day (means, property DayOpened is false and a receipt is going to be started by calling beginFiscalReceipt), it must check whether the application host has the same date and time as the fiscal printer.

  • If there is a time difference less than a predefined changeable application parameter then a synchronization has to take place automatically (default value for the changable application parameter is 15 minutes).

  • If there is a time difference higher than the defined changeable application parameter then the POS application is required to display a message regarding like e.g.:

    Time and date difference between POS and Fiscal Printer
POS time is: DD.MM.YYYY HH:MM
Fiscal printer time is: DD.MM.YYYY HH:MM
Do you want to synchronize POS date and time to the Fiscal Printer?"

  • If No → date and time are not synchronized

  • If Yes → date and time from POS are synchronized to Fiscal Printer

    1. POS application must check if the fiscal day is closed, and after this must do the synchronization.

    2. If the fiscal day is open, the POS application must prompt a message "Can’t not set time in fiscal day open"

Note

In case of an attempt to set the date and time more than 2 days ahead in the future, this is refused by the fiscal printer.

  • Date and Time synchronization must be done each time the application starts, cashier sign on, fiscal day opens.

  • This procedure to be done using special privileges – to be blocked for cashiers, but to be allowed to supervisors.

  • This system of synchronization must be implemented in a way to avoid blocking of sales.

Synchronization of VAT Rates [MANDATORY]

VAT rates have to be synchronized between POS application and Fiscal Printer using the methods setVatValue and setVatTable. Only when `setVatTable`is called, the changed VAT rates are written to the fiscal printer device.

VAT rates must be synchronized for all the entire chain: back office store server → fiscal POS application → fiscal printer.

VAT rates may be altered at the fiscal printer only in fiscal day is closed. That means, the POS application may alter the VAT Rates before the first legally relevant receipt (fiscal sales receipt or generic fiscal receipt) is started.

When the cashier signs into fiscal POS application, the application must check if it has the same VAT rates set as the fiscal printer.

  • If there is a difference, the POS application needs to display a message regarding like There is a VAT Rates difference between POS and Fiscal Printer

    POS VAT Rates are:
...
Fiscal Printer VAT Rates are:
...
Call the supervisor to synchronize the VAT rates!

  • supervisor privileges needs to be checked before VAT rates synchronization

Note

  • Fiscal printer allows a maximum of 30 VAT rate changes. After this, Fiscal Memory and EJ SD Card must be replaced with new ones in order to allow other changes.

  • If the maximum number VAT rate changes is reached then an exception/error with error code EXTENDED and ExtendedErroCode EFPTR_TECHNICAL_ASSISTANCE is issued. In that case, the POS application must stop the sale and prompt the message to "change the fiscal memory" because the VAT rate changes was unsuccessful.

  • The following VAT rates are enabled by default and may be changed: VAT A to VAT D (0 to 3)

  • VAT Rate E (4):

    • used for items which are excepted from VAT

    • Rate values (%) is always 0.00% - can’t be changed

    • It represent the sales amount of items excepted from VAT

  • VAT Rate F (5):

    • Used for Other Taxed (e.g. Road Tax for fuel items)

    • Rate value (%) is always 0.00% - can’t be changed

    • It represents the value of other taxes

  • VAT Rates G and H (6 and 7):

    • are disabled by default (equal to 99.99%)

    • To enable them, the application needs to set up a different value

  • It is recommended that VAT rate setting is allowed with special privileges only – to be blocked for cashier, but to be allowed to supervisor.

  • To perform a VAT rate change, all 8 VAT Rates must be set by POS application using ´setVatValue` taking into consideration that VAT E and VAT F must be always zero, and if we need VAT G and VAT H to remain disabled they must be equal to 9999.

  • If by mistake, VAT Rates E and F will be send to the printer different by zero, the VAT rates will not be changed.

  • In case the setting of VAT rates fails, a message must be displayed on operator display regarding this error!

CIF Validation Algorithm

  1. CIF represents the company VAT tax number. It contains:

    • "RO" – Fiscal Attribute, used only for company who pay the VAT to the state.

    • " " - Blank Fiscal Attribute for company who do not pay VAT to the state.

    • Company VAT tax number – numeric less than 13 digits.

  2. CIF number structure

    [|ZZZZZZZZZ|] |C|
 |_________|  |_|
     :         :
     :         --> Check digit
     :
     -- > CIF ID (max 9 digits)

  3. The fiscal attribute "RO" is ignored in case it exists.

  4. The CIF number is checked to have maximum length of 10 digits and contain only numeric characters.

  5. The following test key "753 217 532" is used. The CIF number and the test key are inverted. The inverted test key is "235 712 357".

  6. The first digit of the inverted CIF number is ignored (this is the check digit).

  7. Each digit from the inverted CIF is multiplied with the corresponding digit from the inverted test key. (Multiply digit 1 from inverted CIF with digit 1 from inverted test key; multiply digit 2 from inverted CIF with digit 2 from inverted test key, ..)

  8. Sum all results from step 5.

A PHP implementation can be found here: http://ro.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cod_de_Identificare_Fiscal%C4%83

The printer accepts any CIF shorter than 13 characters to be printed, the POS needs to check the CIF so as not to print illegal CIF’s (the law states that the costumer must present a copy of his legal statement that mentions the CIF, the store must make a copy of this statement for later verification, also ANAF will reject any fiscal tickets with illegal CIF).

CNP Validation Algorithm

The CNP has the following form:

|S||AA||LL||ZZ||JJ||ZZZ||C|
|C| => Control Digit
|ZZZ| => Person serial number
|JJ| => County code
|ZZ| => Birth day
|LL| => Birth month
|AA| => Birth year
|S| => Gender (M/F) for:
       1/2 - Romanian citizens born between 1st January 1900 and 31st December 1999
       3/4 - Romanian citizens born between 1st January 1800 and 31st December 1899
       5/6 - Romanian citizens born between 1st January 2000 and 31st December 2099

  • Maximum length of the code is 13 digits.

  • For validation the following key is used: 279146358279.

  • Every digit is multiplied with the corresponding digit of the key.

  • The results of all multiplications are added. The sum will be multiplied by 10 and the result will be divided by 11. MOD 11 will be applied - the result represents the control digit. If the obtained result is 10 then the control digit will be 1 (one), else the control digit is the value of the rest.

  • In order to have a valid CNP, the control digit has to correspond with the last digit of the CNP code.

Special Menu at POS

It’s mandatory for POS application to have a special menu "Fiscal Reports". The menu must be accessable by supervisor cashier only, using security privileges (minimum 4 digits password).

All these reports (except for Z report) can be either be printed on fiscal printer. Or, instead printed the printing content be down loaded to POS application and displayed on operator screen. This may be achieved by calling the directIO `DIRECTIO_MAKE_SOFT_COPY', see Javadoc document [jd-ro] for details.

This menu must contain:

  1. Fiscal X-report (overview daily activity), informal character → printXReport

  2. Fiscal Z-report (overview daily total), official character, the fiscal day will be automatically closed when an Z report is requested → printZReport. This report always printed and cannot be downloaded.

  3. Printer Self-Test → directIO TEST_PRINT_SELFTEST, see Javadoc document [jd-ro] for details. This command prints a self-test including information about the print unit itself, the fiscal status and the EJ device assigned.

  4. Fiscal Date to Date Report – mandatory to support usage of parameters:

    1. Detailed Fiscal Date to Date Report → printReport with reportType=FPTR_RT_DATE

    2. Summary Fiscal Date to Date Report → printPeriodicTotalsReport

    3. There are further filter options to be provided for the content of this reports as requested by law. See directIO XXX for details (Currently not supported by Probase POS' FiscalPrinter implementation):

    4. Reprint tickets from EJ filtered by date and time → directIO EJ_REPRINT_TICKET2TICKET_BY_DATE, see Javadoc document [jd-ro] for details.

    5. Reprint tickets from EJ filtered by Z report number (AKA EOD or session number) → directIO EJ_REPRINT_TICKET2TICKET_BY_SESSION, see Javadoc document [jd-ro] for details.

ZReport - End of Fiscal Day

At the end of a business day, the fiscal day must be closed using a call to printZReport. In that case, fiscal printer will perform the following actions automatically: All the daily counters, totalizers of fiscal printer will be stored in the fiscal memory. After the printing of the Z Report has been finished, it will be appended to the EJ file and the EJ file will be closed internally. An SHA 2 algorithm is used to calculate the Message Digest for the whole EJ file. The Message Digest will also be stored in the fiscal memory and printed as last line of Z Report.

This report displays the turnover performed during the day and the VAT collected, all media payments used during the fiscal day, all counters and totalizers.

Figure 16. Z Report Example

After Z Report has been printed, all the daily counters are reset.

The Z Report for the new EJ fiscal printer contains much more information than the old fiscal printer without EJ. All these new information must be handled by the new version of fiscal POS application (e.g.: media payments, sales with CIF, etc.).

In case PAPER NEAR END sensor is ON, the report will not be printed.

In case the Z report is interrupted for any reason, the application has to perform a clearError or a resetPrinter(the last is preferred if the interruption was caused by an power fail).

The Z Report may be issued:

  • manually by the POS application – from a special menu on the POS side [MANDATORY]

  • automatically by the server during the business end of day process [OPTIONAL]

  • manually by the POS application, if 24 hours has elapsed since the first fiscal ticket of the currently running fiscal day. [MANDATORY]
    In that case it’s not allowed to sale those 24 hours has elapsed. The 24 hours is counted by fiscal printer. The fiscal printer will return an error/exception with code E_EXTENDED and ExtendedErrroCode EFPTR_DAY_END_REQUIRED in case any attempt is made to continue sales after the 24 hours has elapsed. The POS application must display an appropriate message on the screen, e.g. "24 hours elapsed, Z Report is required!"

At every EOD, an XML file created based on Z Report will be generated by fiscal printer in order to be send to Fiscal Authority server. This file must be saved to cover the cases when the connection is offline and the file needs to be transmitted later on using other ways. The saved procedure is not required to be implemented in the POS application! It will be in charge of CoMo to do an automatic save of the XML file on the path defined by the customer.

Scenario:

  • Before installation of the printer, it will be discussed with customer where they want to saved XML file to. They must define a path on POS or back office store server or country server or in the cloud.

  • This path will be setup inside of CoMo LAN parameters.

  • After every EOD, the CoMo will be in charge to create the XML file, to apply the digital signature and save the file at the pre-defined path.

Homologation - Fiscal Certification

Every fiscal system (fiscal printer device, customer display, POS till, POS application) has to pass a fiscal homologation / fiscal certification that is performed by the Romanian Fiscal Authorities on the fiscal solution. Starting with the Electronic Journal law, a new institute was put in charge to issue fiscal certifications. The institute is the National Institute for Research and Development in Informatics (ICI).

The homologation / fiscal certification will allow:

  • The fiscal solution to be distributed and used in Romanian market

  • The installation of the fiscal solution in retailer’s stores.

  • The maintenance of the fiscal solution

During the homologation / fiscal certification, a lot of tests will be performed in order to check that the fiscal solution fulfills the Romanian fiscal regulations, also the new introduced regulation regarding Electronic Journal and Data Transmission to Fiscal Authority.

The fiscal solution (hardware and software) has to be sold by a Romanian company. It is required that the software provider sells the software licenses for the fiscal POS application to a fiscal equipment distributor from Romania. The distributor needs an invoice from the software provider in order to complete the fiscal solution kit, otherwise the fiscal solution is incomplete and cannot be sold and installed.

Subsequently, the retailer will have to buy the complete fiscal solution (hardware and software) from fiscal equipment distributor in order to use it.

Once the homologation / fiscal certification is finished, the POS application can’t be modified anymore. The new EJ fiscal law imposes a new technical certification in case modification of the POS application and the fiscal firmware of the fiscal printer device are needed.

Homologation / Fiscal Certification Steps

  1. Provide a translated user guide in Romanian

  2. Documentation for POS application in Romanian language – like user manual for cashier (including technical descriptions and interaction cash desk display + cash desk + Printer + receipt)

  3. All messages, buttons etc. in Romanian language

  4. Provide a hardware platform with the fiscal kit, the till and the POS application

  5. Provide a sealed envelope with a CD containing the POS application version to be homologated

  6. Provide fiscal documents related to the Fiscal controller (provided by the integrator)

  7. ICI (Institute) will test on their own:

    1. all fiscal function

    2. all EJ functions

    3. all Data Transmission functions

    4. empty memory

    5. memory near full

    6. memory full

    7. empty EJ SD Card

    8. EJ SD Card near full

    9. EJ SD Card full

    10. Error handling in different areas of the receipts and reports

    11. Overflow tests

  8. The tests will perform electrical shutdown of the different peripherals in different states of an open fiscal ticket.

  9. Tests for missing paper, cover open of fiscal printer in different states of an open fiscal ticket.

  10. The test dates have to be planned at least 2 weeks in advance.

  11. After technical homologation / fiscal certification, the fiscal authorization for delivery, installing and service of the homologated system will be obtained from the Ministry of Finance.

  12. After fiscal authorization from Ministry of Finance, the fiscal solution can be sold and used in the store.

Administrative Information

  1. It will be retailer’s responsibility to store and archive:

    1. Z Report on paper for 5 years

    2. Fiscal memory for 10 years

    3. Electronic Journal Media (SD Card) for 5 years

  2. There are two special books which belongs to each printer

    1. Book of service technician intervention (book for fiscal maintenance )

    2. Register (emergency book – manual sales written into the Register in case of failure to work of fiscal solution )

  3. RBC can offer a solution to archive fiscal memory, EJ SD Cards and also the two paper books.

  4. To perform some operations on fiscal printers, procedures have to be performed by sub-contractors approved by the Romanian fiscal authorities:

    1. importing from abroad fiscal products

    2. delivery on the Romanian market of fiscal products

    3. installation of the printer with initialization of the fiscal memory, electronic journal and Data Communication Module (data transmission module)

  5. The printer can be moved from one location to another as long as the CIF number remains the same and it’s in the same county/district. EJ printers have the possibility to change the address inside the fiscal header. No more than 16 fiscal header changes are allowed, however.

  6. During the fiscalization procedure the printer receives a fiscal ID, which is paired with the manufacturer serial number of the fiscal printer for the lifetime period of the device.

  7. All the movements from one location to another of the fiscal devices starting with the import or production in Romania must be registered at the Fiscal Authorities in the Electronic Register of AMEF (fiscal devices).

Fiscal Printer Working Modes

Important
 These modes must only be set by the service technician, not by the application.

According with the law, the printer can work in different modes:

  • Fiscal ticket and Z Report for standard operation - Working mode = DEFAULT (0).
    This is the normal operational mode.

  • Fiscal ticket and Z Report for TEST – Working mode = TEST (1).
    This mode is used for the first fiscal ticket and first Z report after fiscalization of the printer.

  • Fiscal ticket and Z Report for PROBA – Working mode = PROBA (2).
    This mode is used for the fiscal tickets and Z reports issued by Service Technician in order to test the printer after a service intervention.

  • Fiscal ticket and Z Report for INITIERE – Working mode = INITIERE (3).
    This mode is used for fiscal tickets and Z reports issued when the service technician is training new cashiers in real mode.

The current working mode may be fetched using the directIO GET_WORKING_MODE.

Important
 If the working mode is not in standard mode (DEFAULT), it is recommended to display on the cashier screen the current working mode: TEST, PROBA or INITERE.
Note
 Do not mix those working modes up with the ordinary training mode (started with beginTraining method). The application should trigger the ordinary training mode only! The training mode is only available before the fiscal day has been started.
